# PART 1 – V1 Vision of Carindex (reduced version)

## What Carindex V1 Really Does

Carindex V1 is a **sourcing and pricing tool for used car dealers** that:

1. **Aggregates used car listings** from 2-3 main sources (AutoScout24, mobile.de, LeBonCoin)
2. **Calculates a simple market price** based on found comparables
3. **Allows quick search** for vehicles with essential filters
4. **Sends email alerts** when new listings match criteria

**In summary**: An intelligent search engine + a market price indicator, nothing more.

---

## What Carindex V1 Will NOT Do

**Intentionally excluded to maintain focus**:

- ❌ **B2B Marketplace**: no intermediation, no transactions
- ❌ **Complex leadgen**: no lead scoring, no automatic qualification
- ❌ **Data reports for manufacturers**: no enterprise reports, no consulting
- ❌ **Complete public API**: no DMS/CRM integrations at start
- ❌ **Advanced stock analysis**: no turnover, no predictions
- ❌ **Complex multi-country**: 1-2 countries max at start (France + 1 other)
- ❌ **Multi-country arbitrage**: too complex for V1
- ❌ **Advanced analytics dashboard**: no complex KPIs, no advanced charts

**Why**: Each excluded feature = less code to maintain, fewer bugs, more focus on what generates immediate value.

---

## The 3 Main Promises for a Used Car Dealer

### 1. "Find vehicles faster"
**Problem**: A dealer spends hours searching manually on multiple sites.  
**Solution**: One search on Carindex = results from 2-3 sites at once.

### 2. "Know if the price is good"
**Problem**: Hard to know if a vehicle is well-priced without manual comparison.  
**Solution**: Carindex automatically calculates a market price based on comparables.

### 3. "Don't miss any opportunity"
**Problem**: Good deals go fast, impossible to monitor 24/7.  
**Solution**: Automatic email alerts as soon as a new listing matches criteria.

---

## Simple Sentence to Explain Carindex on the Phone

> **"Carindex is like having an assistant who monitors AutoScout24, mobile.de and LeBonCoin for you. As soon as a listing matches what you're looking for, you receive an email. And for each vehicle, we tell you if the price is in the market average or not."**

**Why it works**:
- Concrete (assistant, email, price)
- Understandable in 30 seconds
- Solves a real problem (wasting time searching)

# PART 2 – V1 Product Scope (features)

## 1) Must have (absolutely necessary to sell)

### Multi-criteria Search
**What it's for**: Allows quick vehicle search by brand, model, year, price, mileage, fuel, location.  
**Why priority**: It's the core feature. Without effective search, the product has no value.

### Basic Market Price
**What it's for**: Displays an average price calculated from similar vehicles found in database.  
**Why priority**: It's the main differentiator. A dealer wants to know if a vehicle is well-priced before calling.

### Simple Alerts (email)
**What it's for**: Sends an email when a new listing matches saved search criteria.  
**Why priority**: Creates usage habit. A dealer who receives useful alerts = customer who stays.

### Basic Signup / Login
**What it's for**: Allows creating account, saving searches, managing alerts.  
**Why priority**: Without user account, impossible to monetize or personalize experience.

### Minimal Dashboard
**What it's for**: Displays recent searches, active alerts, results overview.  
**Why priority**: Gives impression of "finished" product even if minimal.

---

## 2) Should have (important but can come after launch)

### Market Price Confidence Index
**What it's for**: Displays a percentage (ex: 75%) indicating reliability of calculated market price.  
**Why not must have**: Market price alone already works. Confidence index improves credibility but isn't blocking.

### Simple Price History
**What it's for**: Shows price evolution of a listing if followed for several days.  
**Why not must have**: Useful but not essential for a dealer looking to buy quickly.

### Advanced Filters (version, trim, color)
**What it's for**: Allows refining search with additional criteria.  
**Why not must have**: Basic filters (brand, model, price, km) are enough to start.

### Results Export (CSV/Excel)
**What it's for**: Allows downloading search results for external processing.  
**Why not must have**: Nice to have for some clients, but not critical.

### Basic Stock Analysis
**What it's for**: Compares dealer stock with market (ex: "you have 5 BMW 320d, market has 200").  
**Why not must have**: Useful for dealerships, but not essential for small dealers.

---

## 3) Won't have for V1 (will be for later)

### Complete Public API
**Why not now**: Complex to maintain, requires documentation, technical support. Can sell without API at start.

### Advanced Reports / Analytics
**Why not now**: Dealers want to find vehicles first, not analyze trends. That's for later.

### Advanced Stock Analysis (turnover, predictions)
**Why not now**: Too complex, requires lots of historical data. Not priority.

### Marketplace or Integrated Arbitrage
**Why not now**: Completely changes product model. Too risky for V1.

### Complex Multi-Country (more than 2 countries)
**Why not now**: Each country = new sources, new rules, more complexity. Focus on 1-2 countries first.

### DMS/CRM Integrations
**Why not now**: Very long sales cycle, need dedicated technical support. Not for V1.

### Automatic Vehicle Scoring
**Why not now**: Requires ML, lots of data, complex validation. Too early.

## Strategy to Increase Prices Later

### "Grandfathering" Method
**Principle**: Existing customers keep their price, new customers pay new price.

**Example**:
- Months 1-6: Starter €99, Pro €299
- Month 7+: Starter €149, Pro €399
- Existing customers: Stay at €99 / €299

**Advantages**:
- No churn related to increase
- Existing customers feel privileged
- New customers accept new price

### Communication
> "We're increasing our prices for new customers starting [date]. As an existing customer, you keep your current price. Thanks for your trust!"

### Alternative: "Voluntary Upgrade"
**Principle**: Propose existing customers move to new price in exchange for benefits.

**Example**:
- "Move to €149 and receive 3 months of free premium alerts"
- "Move to €399 and receive free personalized setup"

**Advantages**:
- Some customers accept voluntarily
- No churn
- Increases LTV

# PART 5 – Minimal Technical Focus

## Scraping

### How Many Sources at Start
**2 sources max**:
1. **LeBonCoin** (France) - Priority #1
2. **AutoScout24** (France) - Priority #2

**Why not more**:
- Each source = new code to maintain
- Each source = new potential bugs
- Better 2 well-scraped sources than 5 poorly-scraped sources

### Simple Frequency Strategy
**Incremental scraping**: Only scrape new listings, not entire catalog.

**Frequency**:
- **New listings**: Every 6-8 hours
- **Price updates**: Every 12 hours
- **Status check**: Every 24 hours (active/expired)

**Why**:
- Reduces Apify costs
- Reduces server load
- Sufficient to have fresh data

### Minimum to Not Explode Infrastructure
**Limits**:
- Max 100K listings in database (France)
- Scraping by segments (top 20 brands first)
- Redis cache to avoid repeated queries
- Bull queue to manage asynchronous tasks

**Infrastructure costs**:
- Apify: €200-400/month (scraping)
- Supabase: €50-100/month (database)
- Redis: €20-50/month (cache)
- **Total: €270-550/month**

---

## Database

### Simple Model (without detailing everything)

**Table `listings`**:
- ID, source, URL, brand, model, year, price, km, fuel, location, images, description, dates

**Table `price_history`**:
- ID, listing_id, price, date

**Table `market_stats`**:
- ID, brand, model, year, avg_price, median_price, nb_comparables, calculation_date

**Table `users`**:
- ID, email, plan, created_at

**Table `alerts`**:
- ID, user_id, criteria (JSON), active, created_at

**That's it.** Don't need more for V1.

### What's Necessary for Search
**Essential indexes**:
- `listings(brand, model, year)`: For fast search
- `listings(price, mileage)`: For price/km filters
- `listings(location_country, location_city)`: For geographic search
- `listings(created_at)`: To sort by date

**Full-text search**: Typesense or PostgreSQL `tsvector` for text search (description, etc.)

### What's Necessary for Market Price
**Simple algorithm**:
1. Find comparables (same brand/model, year ±2, km ±20%)
2. Calculate average + median of prices
3. Adjust according to mileage if necessary
4. Return market price + number of comparables

**No need for ML or complex algorithm**: A weighted average already works very well.

**What it means**:
